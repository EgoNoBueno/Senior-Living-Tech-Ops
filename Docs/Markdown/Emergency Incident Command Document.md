## Purpose
This document outlines the expectations and requirements for emergency preparedness as per the CMS Emergency Preparedness Final Rule and E-Tag guidelines. It serves as a guide for establishing an Emergency Incident Command structure and ensuring compliance with regulatory standards.

## Key Elements of Emergency Preparedness

### 1. Emergency Preparedness Program
- **Comprehensive Approach**: Address health, safety, and security needs of the facility, staff, patients, and community.
- **Four Core Elements**:
  1. Emergency Plan based on a Risk Assessment.
  2. Policies and Procedures.
  3. Communication Plan.
  4. Training and Testing Program.

### 2. Definitions
- **Emergency**: A hazard impact requiring enhanced capacity and incident command processes.
- **Disaster**: A larger-scale hazard impact with outcomes below expectations despite enhanced capacity.

### 3. Survey Protocols
- Compliance assessed during initial, revalidation, recertification, and complaint surveys.
- Integration with Life Safety Code (LSC) surveys.

### 4. Incident Command Structure
- Establish a clear chain of command.
- Define roles and responsibilities for all staff.
- Ensure coordination with local emergency management agencies.

### 5. Communication Plan
- Maintain updated contact information for staff, patients, and emergency services.
- Establish protocols for internal and external communication during emergencies.

### 6. Training and Testing
- Conduct regular training sessions for staff on emergency procedures.
- Perform drills and exercises to test the effectiveness of the Emergency Plan.

## Compliance with E-Tag Expectations
- Ensure all emergency preparedness activities align with E-Tag requirements.
- Document all training, testing, and updates to the Emergency Plan.
